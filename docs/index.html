Masjid Call Block — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 25, 2026

Masjid Call Block is built to silence notifications and phone calls from everyone except the contacts you choose, while a single toggle is on. This policy explains, plainly, what the app does and doesn't do with your data.

What stays on your device, always

Your contacts, the list of people you've allowed through, and your blocked-call log are stored only in this app's local storage on your phone. None of it is ever uploaded, synced to a server, or shared with us or anyone else. There is no account, no sign-in, and no backend that this data is sent to.

What the app accesses, and why

  • Contacts — to let you pick who's on your allowed list, and to match incoming calls/notifications against it. Read-only, never leaves your device.
  • Notifications (Notification Listener access) — to silence notifications from apps other than your allowed contacts while the toggle is on. The app reads notification content only to decide whether to cancel it; nothing is stored or transmitted off-device.
  • Phone calls (Call Screening) — to silence calls from numbers not on your allowed list. Calls are never recorded or sent anywhere.
  • Do Not Disturb access — to keep your ringer silent for everyone except allowed contacts while the toggle is on.

Anonymous usage analytics

The app uses Firebase Analytics to log a small number of anonymous, aggregate events so we can tell whether the app is working as intended:

  • onboarding_complete — the first-time setup was finished
  • focus_mode_toggled — the on/off toggle was flipped (and which state)
  • call_blocked / notification_blocked — something was silenced

These events are counts and on/off states only. They never include a contact's name, phone number, or any other detail from your contacts or call log. This data is processed by Google as our analytics provider and is not sold or shared for advertising.

No ads, no data sale

Masjid Call Block has no ads and does not sell or share your data with third parties.
